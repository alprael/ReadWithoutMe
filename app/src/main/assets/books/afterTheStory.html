After the Story

by the best guy every

Alex Rael

What a wonderful time that all was. It was super fun, I was impressed with myself, and I learned so much. What a crazy adventure.

"Well, I guess it's time to go home." I told myself exhaustingly.

I worked so hard to accomplish what I set out to do, and I did it. Home seemed to be the next great adventure of mine to impart on. Before laying down for th night, I decided to eat dinner. I'm not a good cook, so I ordered take out instead. A nice grilled chicken breast ontop of some rice and vegetables made for an incredible post evening meal.

As I lay down and stare at the ceiling, I can't stop thinking about how the day went and everything that occured. I don't believe it magic, but it all seemed magical to me. The places I went, and the people I met. It was all very fun and overwhelming.

"I don't think I've ever accomplished anything as important than this in my life." I reassurringly told myself.

How could I let this day end. I was a hero, and my actions mattered. Just yesterday I was sitting down watching Netflix all day. I was influential, and people looked up to me today. Like I said, it was all magical.

I continued to look at the ceiling...

Well then...

I guess it's time to move on. I mean, it's nice to have at least one day that's really worth living, but nothing really changed. I still have a job to go to, and family to see. The same old routine.

I wonder if I can consider those people my friends. They really wouldn't mind seeing me again, but am I really welcome whenever I please? They'd say yes, but would that be true? Honestly, I don't think my life could ever again be as exciting as today was.  My entire lively hood lived out in just one day. Today is the best it probably will ever be for me.

And now I'm alone, at home, ready to continue on with my same, unchanged, routine of a life.

I lay here on my bed feeling a bit insecure, at home...with nothing to do except move on.

I continue to think, and think. I lay here, at home, on top of a vast planet with billions of years of development, circling around the largest star in our solar system surrounded by the vast cosmos of the expanding universe, but I'll keep thinking and feeling insecure. 

"Does this all really matter? What today, yesterday or even tomorrow?"

....

"Probably not. Oh well, time to go to bed, sleep and wake up again to do the same old routine. Do as I usualy do, and live on unmeaningfully."
